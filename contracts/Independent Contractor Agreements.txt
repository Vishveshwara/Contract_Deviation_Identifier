12.1 Retain Books and Records

Each party shall keep its Books and Records (including personnel files) that relate to this agreement, and maintain them in a manner reasonably consistent with prior practices.

12.2 Inspection and Audit

On reasonable notice from the other party, each party shall provide the other party, and the other party's Representatives, reasonable access to its Books and Records that relate to this agreement (and allow the other party to make photocopies, at the other party's expense), during normal business hours.

12.3 Corrections

If either party's audit reveals errors or discrepancies in the other party's Books and Records, the other party shall promptly correct the error or discrepancy, including refunding any over-payments or making up any under-payments.

13 Intellectual Property

13.1 Ownership of Work Product

The parties hereby acknowledge that the Work Product, and all documentation, information, systems, and other results developed in connection with the Work Product, will, to the extent permitted by Law, be a "work made for hire" within the definition of Section 101 of the Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. 101) and will remain #PARTY A#'s exclusive property.

13.2 Assignment of Work Product

If and to the extent that any Work Product is not deemed to be a work made for hire within the definition of the Copyright Act, at the completion and acceptance of the #DELIVERABLES#, #PARTY B# shall promptly assign to #PARTY A# all its right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including any Intellectual Property rights.

13.3 Cooperation

During and after the Term, on #PARTY A#'s reasonable request, #PARTY B# shall provide all assistance necessary and desirable for #PARTY A# to establish, preserve, and enforce its rights, title, and interest in and to the Work Product.

13.4 Moral Rights

At the completion and acceptance of the #DELIVERABLES#, #PARTY B# hereby irrevocably waives any moral rights it has in the Work Product, to the maximum extent permitted by Law.

14.2 Non-Solicitation of Employees

15 Non-Competition

15.1 No Competition

During the Term and for #NON-COMPETITION DURATION# months after the termination of this agreement, #PARTY B# shall not, without #PARTY A#'s prior written consent, (a) directly or indirectly engage in, become interested in, or be connected with (as a stockholder, director, officer, employee, partner, member, manager, principal, agent, Representative, or consultant), or (b) manage, operate, control, be employed by, participate in, consult with, or otherwise furnish any services or assistance to any business or entity that provides products or services that are the same as, or substantially similar to, those products or services provided by #PARTY A#.

15.2 Exclusion

The restrictions in this section 15 do not apply to:

(a) #PARTY B#'s ownership of less than #15% of the outstanding stock of a publicly traded company or any other public company,
(b) services provided to #PARTY A# under this agreement, or
(c) the business of #PARTY B# (and its Affiliates) as conducted as of the Effective Date and provided it does not expand its business during the Term or at any time during the Non-Competition Period.
